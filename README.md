## Research Contributions
#### Kirispoon: A Pivot-Based Kirigami Utensil for Hand-Held and Robot-Assisted Feeding (IROS 2026 Publication)
Summary:
The Kiri-Spoon is a 3D-printed assistive device that can be handheld or robot-operated. Utilizing shape-changing technology, the spoon morphs from 2D to 3D to secure food for transport. This compliant mechanism adapts to diverse food textures and geometries, showcasing a novel application of soft robotic mechanical intelligence in assistive technology.

I enjoyed working on this project while working in undergraduate research at Virginia Tech under the [Collab](https://collab.me.vt.edu/). Check out the [IROS publication](https://collab.me.vt.edu/pdfs/keone_iros2026.pdf) and [Kirispoon Github](https://github.com/Kiri-Spoon/Kiri-Spoon) for more information on this project!
